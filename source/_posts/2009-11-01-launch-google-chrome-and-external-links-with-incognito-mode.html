--- layout: post title: Launch Google Chrome and external links with incognito mode. tags: - chrome - google - quickfire - tips status: publish type: post published: true meta: _edit_last: '4' --- The instructions floating around are to place the --incognito switch (single - works fine for me) in your desktop shortcut. But sometimes you may be in an application like Tweek Deck, etc that links to webpages. These pages don't launch in incognito if you only fix your desktop shortcut. Here is how to do both. Google Chrome is set as the default browser in this case. (These instructions are based off of Windows XP) Right-click on your Chrome shortcut. Choose Properties. Add -incognito to the end: 
chrome.exe" --incognito
To launch all web shortcuts and links from your system: Control Panel > Folder Options > File Types Select the File TypeÂ URL:HyperText Transfer Protocol and choose Advanced Select action open and edit. Just like before add the switch to the end like this: 
chrome.exe" --incognito -- "%1"
